Problem & aim

Vehicle ownership is rising sharply (a family may own several vehicles) but road capacity is not added, so traffic incidents & congestion appear. Traffic Impact Assessment (TIA) assesses roadway operation/effectiveness; if the LOS at a junction is poor, the authority can design management strategies to reduce congestion. Raw traffic data is in number format and hard to comprehend, so the study processes it with visual analytics to (1) improve TIA, (2) identify traffic patterns, and (3) develop predictive models for proactive control.

Key concepts & definitions

Visual analytics
An approach that uses graphics to highlight connections in data & disclose hidden patterns. Four design principles for traffic: spatial, temporal, spatial-temporal, multivariate.
PCU / PCE
Passenger Car Unit — normalises different vehicle types to a common unit. Equipment PCU values (Malaysia, JKR): cars 1, motorcycles 0.33, light vans 1.75, medium lorries 1.75, heavy lorries 2.25, buses 2.25.
Saturation flow
The maximum number of vehicles a road/junction can handle — the basis of capacity.
Level of Service (LOS)
A grade (A–F) of road condition derived from the volume-to-capacity (V/C) ratio.

Vehicle mix in Malaysia (per the cited data): ~51.2% cars, 39.0% motorcycles, 0.5% buses, 6.3% goods vehicles, 3% other.

Data & method (preprocessing)

The raw database held ~1.8 million documents (document ID, junction ID, client ID, date, time). Pipeline:

  • Select the informative feature traffic_count_by_lane (richer than plain "traffic_count") and sat_flow (saturation flow) as the capacity parameter.
  • Export to CSV via aggregation; clean for nulls & duplicates.
  • Convert timestamps from UTC → Malaysian time (+8h offset).
  • Multiply vehicle counts per lane by the equipment PCU value to get total PCU per junction; group by day (Mon–Sun) and time (12 a.m.–12 p.m.) to observe patterns. Lanes split into turn-left / go-straight / turn-right per junction.

Junctions were anonymised as Junction A/B/C (confidentiality).

Computing the Level of Service (the quantitative core)

LOS evaluates congestion at a junction using the volume-to-capacity ratio:

V/C ratio = (total PCU per hour, i.e. volume) ÷ (total saturation flow of the 3 lanes, i.e. capacity). The higher the ratio, the worse the service.
V/C ratioLOSCondition
0 – 0.60AFree-flowing traffic
0.61 – 0.70BReasonably free flow
0.71 – 0.80CStable flow
0.81 – 0.90DApproaching unstable
0.91 – 0.99EUnstable, near capacity
> 0.99FForced flow — traffic slows & may break down (severe congestion)

Tables III–V in the paper compute hourly LOS for Junctions A/B/C on 20 Jan 2022 (10:00–20:00). Example: Junction B reached LOS values around 0.63–0.80 (services B–C) at midday.

The visualizations (which chart, why)

ChartPurpose / insight
Heat map (Fig. 2)Average PCU by day×hour — PCU drops ~23:00, lowest 01:00–05:00, rises ~06:00, peaks 12:00–14:00; weekends lower than weekdays.

Stacked bar / scatter / lollipop (Figs. 7–9)Daily vehicle distribution & per-lane vehicle counts (do lanes need expanding?).
Line & ridgeline charts (Figs. 10–15)Incident counts over time & their distribution.

Visual analysis of traffic incidents

Three incident types in the TIA: abnormal discharge (a large volume released onto the road in a short period → congestion), offline device (a signal/camera not functioning → no real-time control), and lane block (a lane obstructed by construction/accident/disabled vehicle). Line & ridgeline charts show counts over time per junction (steeper curve = more incidents).

Evaluation vs existing techniques (Table VI)

Methods were rated on ease of use (1–5): "Visualization, Selection & Analysis of Traffic Flows" (2015) scored 2 (unclear trajectory, minimal chart selection); urban-road density clustering (2019) scored 3; "How Visual Analytics Unlock Insights" (2017) scored 4; this paper scored 4 — traffic volume + incidents visualized with tooltips giving exact values.

Conclusions & limitations

Visual analytics gives a comprehensive understanding of traffic dynamics, enabling identification of congested areas, evaluation of performance across junctions, and analysis of incident rates & effects. It supports data-driven decisions for transportation planning.

Limitation / next step: the work is descriptive/diagnostic visual analytics; the authors call for layering predictive models on top for proactive traffic control and congestion mitigation — making management more efficient and safer.

Course link & exam angle

Exam angle: "Explain how LOS is derived from the V/C ratio and which visualizations were used." Answer: LOS = volume (total PCU/hour) ÷ capacity (saturation flow), graded A (≤0.60, free flow) → F (>0.99, breakdown); visualizations = heat maps (spatial-temporal), parallel-coordinate charts (turn movements), bar plots (LOS), each revealing peak/off-peak & weekday/weekend differences.
