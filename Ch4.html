Data Warehousing & Business Performance Management

Key idea

Core message: The data warehouse is BI's cleansed, integrated, historical backbone. ETL feeds it, dimensional modelling + OLAP make analysis fast, and a performance-management layer (BPM, KPIs, scorecards, Six Sigma) turns analysis into managed action.

Data warehouse & data marts

Data Warehouse (DW): a collection of integrated, subject-oriented databases supporting DSS, where each unit is non-volatile and tied to a moment in time — a repository of cleansed, standardized relational data (Inmon).
ComponentMeaning / usage
Dependent data martSubset created directly from the DW.
Independent data martSmall standalone DW for one unit/department.
ODS (operational data store)Interim staging area for the DW.
EDWEnterprise DW — for the whole organisation.
Metadata"Data about data" — describes DW contents & use.

DW characteristics (each explained)

CharacteristicMeaning
Subject-orientedOrganised around business subjects (customer, product, sales) — not applications.
IntegratedConsistent naming, formats, units across all sources (the hard part of ETL).

Architectures & development models

3-tier: data-acquisition (back-end) → the data warehouse → client (front-end) for access & analysis. Five alternatives: independent marts · data-mart bus (conformed dimensions) · hub-and-spoke (corporate information factory) · centralized · federated.

Third option: hosted (cloud) data warehouses — minimal infrastructure investment, frees capacity & cash flow.

ETL — the integration backbone

  • Extract — pull from heterogeneous sources (OLTP DBs, files, APIs) into a staging area / ODS.
  • Transform — clean, standardise, deduplicate, derive, conform dimensions, apply business rules (the bulk of the work).
  • Load — write into the DW; full vs incremental loads (CDC = change data capture).
  • Variant ELT loads raw then transforms inside a powerful warehouse/lake. Related: EAI (push source data in), EII (real-time virtual integration).

Dimensional schemas & OLAP

A fact table holds numeric measures (revenue, qty) + foreign keys; dimension tables hold descriptive context (Product, Store, Date, Customer). The grain = the detail level of one fact row.

  • Star schema — single denormalized dimension tables; fewer joins → fastest; some redundancy.
  • Snowflake schema — dimensions normalized into sub-tables; less redundancy, more joins → slower.

OLAP operations on the cube

  • Slice — subset on one dimension · Dice — subset on multiple dimensions.
  • Drill down / up — move between summarized & detailed.
  • Roll up — aggregate up a dimension · Pivot — rotate the report.

Business Performance Management (BPM) & KPIs

BPM (also CPM/EPM/SEM) alerts managers to opportunities/problems/threats and empowers response via models & collaboration — a closed loop that fixes the strategy-to-execution gap:

1 Strategize→2 Plan→3 Monitor/Analyze→4 Act/Adjust
  • KPI = strategic objective + a metric measured against a goal.
  • Outcome KPIs = lagging (e.g. revenue); Driver KPIs = leading (e.g. sales leads) — you need both.
  • Good KPIs are SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).

Balanced Scorecard (Kaplan & Norton, 1992)

A strategic management system with four perspectives linked by a strategy map (cause & effect): investment in Learning & Growth → better Internal Processes → higher Customer satisfaction → stronger Financial results.

PerspectiveStrategic question
FinancialHow do we look to shareholders?
CustomerHow do customers see us?
Internal Business ProcessWhat must we excel at?
Learning & GrowthCan we keep improving & creating value?
Dashboard vs scorecard: a dashboard is operational/tactical (many real-time indicators, "how are we doing now?"); a scorecard is strategic (periodic objectives vs targets, "are we doing the right things?"). BSC is a scorecard methodology.

Six Sigma

Reduce defects toward 3.4 DPMO (defects per million opportunities ≈ 99.99966% good) via the DMAIC loop: Define → Measure → Analyze → Improve → Control (DMADV designs a new process). DPMO = defects/(units × opportunities) × 1,000,000.

Balanced Scorecard = strategic management system; Six Sigma = performance measurement system. Often paired: BSC sets what to improve, Six Sigma how.

Application cases
