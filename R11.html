Social Intelligence and Web Mining for Renewable Energy Insights

Lara Mwafaq, Mehdi Muhemed Mool, Shahd Munther Mahmoud, Aqeel Mahmood Jawad & Abdalfattah Sharad (Baghdad universities, Iraq) · JOIV: Int. J. Informatics Visualization, 9(6), Nov 2025, pp. 2656–2664.

One-sentence takeaway: mining 12 months of online discourse, the study uses deep-learning sentiment analysis + topic modelling + geotagged regional analysis and feeds them into a regression model that forecasts regional renewable-energy adoption — finding discourse largely positive (58%), dominated by economic & technological themes, with big regional variation.

Problem & aim

The shift to renewable energy depends not only on technology but on public acceptance, awareness & perception — yet most prior research is technical, neglecting human/social factors. Relevant opinion is scattered as unstructured online data (social media, forums, news). Aim: use social intelligence + web mining to interpret this discourse — gauge sentiment, find prevalent themes, and predict adoption patterns per region — turning opinion into policy-level insight.

Key concepts & definitions

Social intelligence
Studying collective human behaviour & emotion to understand society's attitude toward a technology.
Web mining
A data-mining sub-field that extracts knowledge from unstructured web resources (text/images/multimedia) to find trends, sentiment & misinformation.
Sentiment analysis
Classifying opinion as positive / neutral / negative — here via a fine-tuned RoBERTa-large transformer.
Topic modelling (LDA)
Latent Dirichlet Allocation to extract latent themes; optimal topic count chosen by coherence (Cv = 0.67 → 5 topics).

Data & collection

A composite dataset aggregated over Jan–Dec 2024 (1.5M entries) from three source types:

  • Social media (Twitter, Reddit) via OAuth2 API — time-stamped posts, hashtags, geo-locations.
  • Web news portals scraped with BeautifulSoup + XPaths (headline, body, date, region).
  • Energy forums (EnergyCentral, CleanTechnica) via rotating-user-agent crawlers.

Preprocessing: spam/bot filtering (entropy heuristics — entries with entropy <1.0 dropped), text normalization (lowercase, lemmatize, strip emoji), language filtering (LangDetect → English only), duplicate removal (cosine similarity on TF-IDF, threshold 0.92). Cleaned corpus = 900,000 high-quality entries.

Method — a 5-step, multi-stage framework

  1. Data Collection — APIs + scraping + crawling.
  2. Data Preprocessing — the cleaning pipeline above.
  3. Analytical Modeling — sentiment (RoBERTa-large, classes +1/0/−1) + topic modelling (LDA, 5 topics).
  4. Predictive Modeling — multivariate ridge regression (regularisation λ = 0.01 to fight multicollinearity) forecasting adoption rate.
  5. Algorithmic Implementation — HuggingFace Transformers, Gensim, Scikit-learn.

Sentiment results

Across the 900k entries: 58% positive, 27% neutral, 15% negative — a clear positive bias toward renewable energy. Positive entries were longer (more elaborated).

Economic + technological themes dominate (57% combined). Regional sentiment (geotagged): Europe 0.60 (optimism), North America 0.54 (confidence), Asia-Pacific 0.39 (curiosity), South America 0.35 (hesitation), Africa 0.26 (concern — highest negative, 20%).

Predictive model — adoption forecast

A region-specific projection of 5-year adoption was built from sentiment score + economic-theme prevalence + a normalized socioeconomic-development index (GDP/capita, education, existing infra). Forecast adoption: Europe 15.5%, North America 13.4%, Asia-Pacific 10.9%, South America 8.5%, Africa 6.3%. Validated by MAE & RMSE (all errors < 3%) plus a novel Forecast Stability Index (mean 0.88) — confirming sentiment/discourse are effective proxies for adoption.

Conclusions & limitations

Combining social intelligence + web mining turns mass unstructured discourse into a data-driven, forward-looking picture of renewable-energy readiness; large regional variation implies targeted policy & capacity-building. It is framed as a sociotechnical, not purely technical, problem.

Limitations / next steps: only English-language entries (misses non-English/emerging regions); the sentiment model could add affective computing / multimodal emotion; future work = multilingual models + sensor/pricing data.
