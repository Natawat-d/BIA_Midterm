Ch.3 / Ch.4 — descriptive analytics, business reporting & data visualization
One-sentence takeaway: the clinic could not interpret its inventory data (stock imbalance, lost sales), so the authors used VDM + EDA in Tableau to build dashboards that reveal product patterns, sales turnover and efficiency — identifying 3 efficient and 3 inefficient products out of ~98 to guide reorder/stocking decisions.

Problem & research questions

Inventory is a set of policies to control company property (materials/goods for production or resale). The Naavagreen clinic's chief admin reported difficulty understanding inventory data, causing operational disruptions: accumulation of goods, unguaranteed product availability/quality, and losses from decreased sales turnover. Raw data needed to be transformed into comprehensible visual information for decision-making.

The study poses two research questions:

  1. How can the inventory data from Jan 2019 – Dec 2020 be visualized using Tableau?
  2. What insights can be gained from the visualization analysis of this data?

Key concepts & definitions

Inventory / stock
An asset of goods to be sold in the regular business period (plus goods in process / awaiting use). Inventory data monitors available & remaining products.
Data visualization
Transforming data into visual graphs/tables to enable analysis & reporting — "the core of data analysis activities." Stages: acquire, parse, filter, mine, represent, refine, interact.
Visual Data Mining (VDM)
Comprehensible, well-structured process that combines data mining + graphic design + statistics + information visualization to extract patterns visually. Has 3 phases, 8 steps.
Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
Analysing/visualizing data to find its properties, distribution and description (as raw data or easy graphs) so the right analytical method can be chosen — "understand the content and look for patterns."
Tableau
A Business Intelligence software for drag-and-drop visualization, analysis and report creation of large datasets.

The VDM method — 3 phases / 8 steps

(Fig. 1 in the paper.) The work is divided into three main stages:

PhaseStepsWhat happens
1 · Project PlanningStep 1 Justify & plan the project · Step 2 Identify the top business questionShow how to justify/plan a VDM project; map business questions & objectives answerable by visualization/mining.
2 · Data PreparationStep 3 Choose the dataset · Step 4 Transform the dataset · Step 5 Verify the datasetSelect data; clean/transform to remove bias (filter, add/reduce columns, group balance/incoming/sales by month into Excel files); re-check for errors.
3 · Data AnalysisStep 6 Choose visualization/mining tool · Step 7 Analyze the model · Step 8 Verify & presentPick the tool (Tableau), design visualizations to answer the business questions using EDA, then verify the dashboard with stakeholders & present.

The "top business questions" defined

  • Find the sales trend for the last 2 years.
  • Show standard deviation, average, minimum & maximum sales and incoming goods.
  • Show efficient and inefficient products.
  • Show product patterns based on time series.

Tool choice was deliberate: the authors compared Tableau vs Microsoft Power BI (weighing benefits/drawbacks) before choosing Tableau for accurate results.

Data

Inventory data of the Naavagreen Sriwijaya Clinic, Semarang, Jan 2019 – Dec 2020, comprising 98 product items in Excel format, obtained from the clinic's admin. Each record carries beginning balance, incoming goods, and actual sales per product per month (Table I shows an example: products like "Ng anti acne", "Cream natural", "Cream beige", "Project scrub", "Two-way cake with UV"). Transformation grouped these columns by month into separate Excel files for Tableau.

Results & discussion — three dashboards

1 · Product pattern (time series)

Of the 95 products charted, 8 showed the highest fluctuations and 87 had a stable average pattern (incoming goods balanced with sales). The 8 high-fluctuation products: Ng Facial Wash, Ng Skin Toner, Naavagreen Micellar Water, Naavagreen Acne Sunscreen, Naavagreen Brightening Day Serum AP, Ng Moisturizing Sunscreen 1, Naavagreen Silky Loose Powder. Example: Ng Facial Wash had inconsistent incoming goods (Apr 2019–Dec 2020) while sales stayed stable → goods accumulated; 710 pcs leftover stock from 2018, 1,559 pcs leftover from 2019, 995 pcs leftover in 2020.

2 · Sales turnover exploration (2019–2020)

Line chart of sales turnover by standard deviation, average, maximum. After switching the measure to standard deviation, 2020 sales tended to be stable; 2019 was inconsistent. Highest deviation in 2020 was January (4,127,799), with sales turnover 140,134,000, average 1,688,361, maximum 29,348,000. Lowest 2020 deviation in June (2,625,898). In 2019 the highest deviation was May (4,786,526).

3 · Sales turnover pattern (time series)

No consistent pattern. Highest sales in May 2019 at 133,578,500 (+21.82%); lowest in June 2019 at 92,413,000 (−30.82%). In 2020, the highest sales occurred in January at 140,134,000 (+12.86%); lowest in April at 84,147,500 (−28.09%).

Product efficiency — efficient vs inefficient

Efficiency = comparison between output (expenditure) and input (income), i.e. how balanced incoming goods are vs sales. A product is efficient when incoming goods and sales show no significant / balanced difference; inefficient when there is a large discrepancy (over-ordering vs sales).

ProductsEvidence
3 inefficientNg Facial Wash · Ng Skin Toner · Ng Moisturizing Sunscreen 1Largest stock-vs-sales gaps → recommendation to reduce orders in specific months (e.g., reduce Ng Facial Wash incoming in May/Jul/Aug; Ng Skin Toner in Aug/Nov/Dec).
3 efficientNg-Leher Pagi · Ng Badan Pagi · Naavagreen Moist Aha CreamSmall discrepancies — e.g. Ng Leher Pagi: total stock 1,089 vs sales 476 (diff 613); Ng Badan Pagi: 1,043 vs 393 (diff 643); Moist Aha Cream: 1,332 vs 637 (diff 695).

The dashboards give month-by-month reorder recommendations (minimum stock to hold) to prevent accumulation.

Conclusions & limitations

Three dashboards were produced (product patterns, sales turnover, sales efficiency). Of 95 products, 87 were stable and 8 highly fluctuating; the highest percentage increase was January 2020 at 12.86% (turnover IDR 140,134,000); three products were inefficient (Ng Facial Wash, Ng Skin Toner, Ng Moisturizing Sunscreen 1) and three most efficient (Ng-Neher/Leher Pagi, Ng Badan Pagi, Naavagreen Moist Aha Cream). The analysis supports data-driven decisions on stock management and future sales strategy, validated by the clinic's administrative team.

Limitation / next step: the work is purely descriptive (it explains the past). It points toward adding predictive demand forecasting on top of the visual analytics. The "efficient/inefficient" highest-fluctuation selection was done manually by comparing initial balance, incoming goods and sales.
