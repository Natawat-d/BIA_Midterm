Using Sentiment Analysis and CEEMDAN to Learn the Preferences of Consumer Groups: A Case Study of Online Hotel Reviews

Dan Wang, Zaiwu Gong, Guo Wei, María Ángeles Martínez & Enrique Herrera-Viedma (Nanjing Univ. of Info. Sci. & Tech., China; Univ. of North Carolina at Pembroke; Univ. of Granada, Spain) · Applied Soft Computing 191 (2026) 114625.

One-sentence takeaway: a three-stage model learns a consumer group's preferences from noisy, time-varying hotel reviews — BERT gives fine-grained sentiment per criterion, CEEMDAN extracts the group-consistent trend from the sentiment time series, and UTA preference disaggregation infers the group's classification value system (which criteria matter most).

Problem & aim

Online reviews are a rich source of consumer preference, but they are unstructured, time-dependent, and implicitly/ambiguously expressed, overloading both consumers and managers. The paper identifies three gaps: (1) most fine-grained sentiment uses discrete classification, losing the continuity of emotion; (2) traditional MCDM uses explicit/fixed preference scales, missing latent, heterogeneous preferences; (3) prior work ignores the time-dependent nature of reviews. Aim: a data-driven model to learn & interpret consumer-group preferences and infer their value system.

Key concepts & definitions

BERT
Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers — a Transformer-based deep model that reads context in both directions; here BERT-Base-Chinese (12 encoder layers) for fine-grained sentiment.
BERTopic
A BERT-embedding topic model (UMAP → K-Means → MMR → c-TF-IDF) — captures semantics better than LDA; extracts the latent criteria.
CEEMDAN
Complete Ensemble Empirical Mode Decomposition with Adaptive Noise — decomposes a non-stationary time series into Intrinsic Mode Functions (IMFs) + a residual trend; the residual's mean = group-consistent sentiment.
UTA / preference disaggregation
UTilités Additives — an MCDM method that infers an additive value function (criteria weights + thresholds) from examples; variants used: UTADIS, UTAMSCVF, and a criteria-selection model.

Data

Reviews from Ctrip (China's leading OTA) for 15 chain-brand hotels (H1–H15) in the Xinjiekou area of Nanjing, over a full 12-month cycle (1 Dec 2023 – 30 Nov 2024). From 14,133 entries, after preprocessing (standardise text, remove special characters, drop reviews <5 characters, drop reviews whose rating contradicts the text) 10,505 reviews were retained. Each review carries user info, rating, text and timestamp.

Stage 1 — Fine-grained sentiment (BERT + BERTopic)

BERTopic extracted 20 criteria/topics (Table 7): staff attitude, hotel facilities, room type, noise environment, transport convenience, customer satisfaction, breakfast, luggage/checkout, food delivery, hygiene/housekeeping, location, nearby transport, price, tourism services, intelligent facilities, service quality, elevator usage, room comfort, dining/cuisine, sanitary facilities.

BERT-Base-Chinese was fine-tuned to classify sentiment into negative (1) / neutral (2) / positive (3) / unrelated (4). Annotation: 1,500 samples by two experts, inter-annotator kappa 0.82; 9:1 train/test split; Adam optimizer; hyperparameters epoch 10, batch 16, max-seq 48, lr 1e-5. This yields a continuous sentiment score per criterion over time.

Stage 2 — Sentiment signal decomposition (CEEMDAN)

Consumer sentiment over time is multi-scale: a steady group trend + random individual fluctuations. For each criterion's sentiment time series, CEEMDAN adds adaptive white noise, performs EMD, and averages to extract IMFs (short-term/random) + a residual trend (long-term, group-consistent). The mean of the residual trend is taken as the criterion's group-consistent sentiment value — eliminating incidental subjective bias. (CEEMDAN improves on EMD/EEMD by reducing mode mixing & residual noise.)

Stage 3 — Preference modeling (UTA family)

Treating each topic as a criterion and the group-consistent sentiment as its performance value, preference disaggregation infers the group's additive value function and classification value system (which criteria drive the rating class). Three models:

  • UTADIS — finds a value function (with over-/under-estimation errors) that best restores the preference-ordered classes via a linear program.
  • UTAMSCVF — minimises slope changes of the marginal value function → a smoother, more linear (Occam's-razor) value function.
  • Criteria selection model — binary variables pick the most important criteria (a regularisation trade-off controlled by λ).

Outputs include chosen criteria weights (e.g. service quality, room comfort, price) and classification thresholds.

Results

BERT sentiment classification: precision 0.83, recall 0.83, F1 0.83, accuracy 0.835, beating LSTM (0.761) and Random Forest (0.568); Monte-Carlo cross-validation gave a stable mean accuracy ≈ 0.846. The full pipeline produces an interpretable, time-aware value system for the consumer group, letting hotel managers identify needs, allocate resources and make evidence-based decisions.

Conclusions & limitations

The integrated BERT + CEEMDAN + UTA model enhances the interpretability of consumer-group preferences, refines their classification value system, and gives a visual representation of preference structure — more accurate decision support than discrete sentiment + fixed-scale MCDM.

Limitations / next steps (per the paper & method): the residual-trend mean is an assumption-dependent proxy (sensitive to review counts & series length); the approach is computation-heavy. Future directions point to Shapley values & dynamic weighting.
