Key idea

Core message: ~85–90% of corporate data is unstructured text. The whole game is to impose structure on it (the term-document matrix) and then mine that structure for topics, sentiment and web/social patterns.

Text analytics vs text mining vs data mining

  • Text Analytics = Information Retrieval + Information Extraction + Data Mining + Web Mining (or simply IR + Text Mining).
  • Text mining = a semi-automated process of extracting knowledge from unstructured sources (knowledge discovery in textual databases).
  • Data mining vs text mining — both find novel patterns & are semi-automated; the difference is structured (databases) vs unstructured (Word/PDF/XML/HTML) data.

Application areas: information extraction · topic tracking · summarization · categorization · clustering · concept linking · question answering. Domains: law, research, finance, medicine, marketing; email spam filtering & prioritisation.

Terminology & NLP

TermMeaning
Corpus / terms / conceptsThe document collection; the words; higher-level ideas.
StemmingReduce words to root (running→run) — shrinks vocabulary.
LemmatizationMap to dictionary form (better→good).
Stop wordsCommon low-information words (the, is, and) removed.
TokenizingSplit text into terms/tokens (+ POS tagging, morphology).
Term-by-Document Matrix (TDM)Rows = documents, cols = terms, cells = weights — the structure that lets text be mined.
SVD / LSIReduce the sparse TDM's dimensionality (like PCA for text).

NLP (subfield of AI & computational linguistics) moves from the old bag-of-words to "understanding." Challenges: POS tagging, segmentation, word-sense disambiguation, syntactic ambiguity. Resources: WordNet, SentiWordNet.

The three-step text-mining process & TF-IDF

1 Establish the Corpus
collect & digitize→2 Create the TDM
introduce structure→3 Extract Knowledge
classify/cluster/associate

TDM cell values can be raw counts, binary, log frequencies, or TF-IDF:

  • TF (term frequency) — how often a term appears in a document.
  • IDF = log(N / dfₜ) — down-weights terms common across all documents.
  • TF-IDF = TF × IDF — high when a term is frequent here but rare overall ⇒ distinctive. Reduce the huge sparse TDM with SVD.

Topic modeling

Unsupervised discovery of themes. Methods: frequency clustering, LSI/LSA (TF-IDF + SVD, algebraic), and the most popular — LDA (Latent Dirichlet Allocation), a probabilistic generative model: each document = a mixture of topics, each topic = a distribution over words (topics are "latent"). You set the number of topics k (often chosen by a coherence score).

Sentiment analysis

Answers "what do people feel about a topic?" Especially valuable in CRM. Applications: Voice of the Customer / Market / Employee, brand management, financial markets, politics, government intelligence.

Lexicon vs machine learning

ApproachHowTrade-off
Lexicon-basedScore words vs a polarity dictionary (SentiWordNet, VADER)No training data; weak on context/sarcasm
Machine-learningTrain a classifier (NB, SVM, or deep learning / BERT)Accurate & context-aware; needs labelled data

Four-step process

1 Sentiment detection
fact vs opinion (S–O)→2 N–P polarity
negative…positive→3 Target identification
aspect-based→4 Aggregation
Polarity example: "The screen is gorgeous but battery life is terrible." → two targets: screen (P), battery (N). Aggregation rolls word→statement→paragraph→document.

Web mining — three branches

BranchSourceTechniques / usage
Web content miningPage text (HTML)Search engines, IR, sentiment analysis.
Web structure miningURL/hyperlinksPageRank, HITS (hubs & authorities), graph mining.
Web usage miningClicks & sessions (logs/cookies)Clickstream analysis, web analytics, lifetime value.
  • PageRank (Google) — a page is important if important pages link to it; importance flows along links (random-surfer model).
  • HITS — separates hubs (link to many good pages) from authorities (pointed to by many good hubs).

Web analytics metrics: page views, time on site, click paths, traffic sources, visitor profiles, conversion statistics.

Social network analysis (SNA) & social media analytics

SNA = a social structure of actors linked to each other (graph theory + sociology + statistics).

MetricCaptures
Degree centralityNumber of direct connections (popularity)
BetweennessHow often on shortest paths (broker/bridge)
ClosenessNearness to all others (reach)
EigenvectorConnected to well-connected nodes (influence)
Density / clustering coefficientTies present vs possible / how tightly neighbours interconnect

Social media analytics turns Web-2.0 content into competitive insight; engagement ladder: Inactives → Spectators → Joiners → Collectors → Critics → Creators.

Application cases

Insurance group — text mining of bodily-injury claims; AMC Networks — dashboard to predict ratings; "Mining for Lies" — deception detection on 371 statements, 31 cues (LR 67.3%, DT 71.6%, NN 73.5%); Lenovo — customer sentiment + warranty + call-center analysis to redesign products; Tito's Vodka — social-media brand loyalty.
